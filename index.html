02 / The opportunity

Why CBG, why now.

Cannabigerol is the precursor cannabinoid — the molecule the plant converts into THC and CBD as it matures. It accounts for less than 1% of dry weight in conventional flower. That scarcity is exactly what makes it a category opportunity in 2026.

The science is finally catching up

For a decade, CBG sat in CBD's shadow as an academic curiosity. That changed in 2024–2025. The first double-blind, placebo-controlled human trial of isolated CBG (March 2024) showed statistically significant reductions in anxiety and stress without intoxication or cognitive impairment. Penn State's Phase I trial in chemotherapy-induced peripheral neuropathy began enrolling in 2025. The University of Arkansas opened a Phase II trial in ADHD (NCT06115603, currently recruiting). MIRA Pharmaceuticals' preclinical CBG analog matched morphine for inflammatory pain.

This is not hype. It is the standard pre-clinical-to-Phase II progression that built the CBD market a decade earlier. The difference: CBG is non-psychoactive, which removes the cultural and prescriptive friction that THC carries — particularly in pediatric, oncology, and geriatric populations.

The market is moving from CBD-only to multi-cannabinoid

Current market consensus across multiple analyst firms (DelveInsight, Grand View Research, Persistence Market Research) places the global CBG market between USD 150M and USD 1.1B in 2025, with forecast CAGRs ranging from 9.5% to 22% through 2033 depending on definition (isolate-only versus formulated products).

The directional signal matters more than the specific number: over 60% of cannabinoid R&D spend in 2025 targeted minor cannabinoids (CBG, CBN, CBC) over major ones. CBG-dominant product launches grew 145% year-over-year in early-adopter US states. Approximately 42% of new cannabinoid product innovations now incorporate CBG.

The supply bottleneck

Conventional cannabis flower contains less than 1% CBG. Specialized Type IV genetics with inactivated THC and CBD synthase — bred specifically for CBG accumulation — can reach 15–18% CBG content (Hemp Trading × Polytechnic University of Valencia, 2020 published cultivar). Cornell University evaluated 12 CBG-dominant cultivars in 2020, finding most are genetically unstable and require additional breeding cycles to fix the trait. Reliable cultivar sourcing is now the rate-limiting input for the entire category.

This is why we lead with cultivation, not extraction or downstream products: the upstream genetics-to-flower pipeline is where supply is constrained, and where biodynamic cultivation creates a durable margin advantage that competitors with synthetic-biology approaches don't capture.

The CBG opportunity in 2026 is not "discover a new molecule." The molecule is well-characterized. The opportunity is "produce it reliably at agricultural scale, with stable genetics, in a regulatory channel where you can sell it." That is a much narrower bottleneck — and a much more defensible one.

What this tells the investor

Three takeaways:

  1. Real R&D money is flowing. Pharma is running Phase I/II human trials. Biotech raised meaningfully (Demetrix's reported partnerships and ongoing funding rounds; the Amyris × LAVVAN $300M cannabinoid deal; Ginkgo × Cronos $100M; Surterra × Intrexon $100M). The category is well past speculative.
  2. The competition is not in our lane. Demetrix and Willow target high-purity isolate at industrial scale via fermentation — they sell B2B to pharma and personal care brands. Pharma players (InMed, MIRA, Corbus) are running drug-development pipelines aimed at FDA approval. None of these are competing for medical patients in Latin America's largest registered cannabis program.
  3. The genetics gap is real. Cornell's 2020 trial confirmed what experienced growers already know: most "CBG-dominant" cultivars in circulation are genetically unstable. Stable, high-CBG genetics under biodynamic protocols are scarce globally. This is the upstream input we are committed to producing.

Where Loma Verde fits

We are not trying to compete with yeast-fermentation biotech on industrial isolate. We are not trying to compete with InMed on Alzheimer's drug development. We are doing something more focused, more defensible, and far more capital-efficient: cultivating biodynamic CBG flower at small-to-medium scale, on stable genetics, exclusively for an Argentine R&D program serving registered patients.

The biotech-fermentation route makes sense at industrial volumes for high-purity isolate destined for cosmetics or supplements. The botanical-cultivation route makes sense for medical R&D applications where the entourage effect — multiple cannabinoids and terpenes acting synergistically — matters clinically. These are different products for different end uses, not direct substitutes.

04 / Why Argentina

The largest medical cannabis registry in Latin America.

Argentina is not the obvious geography for a cannabis investor in 2026. It should be. The country runs the most established federal patient-cultivation program in Latin America, with over 410,000 registered patients and an unmet waitlist that grows monthly.

The regulatory landscape — what matters

Argentina has two parallel cannabis frameworks. We operate exclusively in the more stable one.

Law 27,350 + REPROCANN (where we operate)

Established 2017, expanded 2020 (Decree 883/2020), reformed 2025 (Resolution 1780/2025). The Ministry of Health runs a national patient registry called REPROCANN. Registered patients can legally cultivate cannabis for personal medical use, or designate authorized third parties — including non-profit civil associations and R&D legal entities — to cultivate on their behalf. Each non-profit can serve up to 150 registered patients.

Our client, Sweet Pacha, is registered as an R&D legal entity under Article 15 of Resolution 1780/2025. This is the channel that 2025 reforms strengthened with stricter physician credentialing, traceability, and quality control — precisely the kind of formalization that creates a durable moat for compliant operators.

Law 27,669 + ARICCAME (not our channel)

Separate 2022 commercial framework targeting industrial hemp and pharmaceutical-grade exports. Created a regulator (ARICCAME) that the current administration dissolved in 2025, leaving this channel in flux. We don't depend on it. Our entire business model operates within the patient-program channel under the Ministry of Health, which has remained operational throughout political transitions.

For the US-aware investor

The closest US analogue to the REPROCANN program is the state-level medical cannabis registry + non-profit caregiver model seen in early-stage states. The economic equivalent is closer to Israel's Ministry of Health-supervised medical program in 2015–2018 — a regulated channel with growing patient base, formal physician credentialing, and a clear supply chain for compliant operators.

Critically, this is not the recreational market. There is no THC dispensary system. Our category is medical-only, prescriber-driven, and structurally protected from the price compression that has hit US recreational margins.

05 / The Loma Verde model

Cultivation as a service. Plant Library as a moat.

We don't own patients, we don't sell to consumers, and we don't take inventory risk. We operate 8,000 m² of certified biodynamic cultivation, exclusively under a service agreement with a licensed R&D principal. The plants belong to the client; we deliver the cultivation expertise.

The structure

Three economic actors, clean separation:

This separation is not bureaucratic theater. It is the legal structure that the 2025 reforms specifically created and reinforced: R&D entities operate alongside non-profit caregivers, with cultivation services explicitly permitted as a delegated function. We were designed for this framework, not retrofitted into it.

Plant Library — the operational moat

Our 8,000 m² site is divided into 120 marked parcels of 1 m² each, plus support infrastructure. Each parcel is dedicated to a specific genetic line, biodynamically managed, and fully traceable from clone to harvest. We call this the Plant Library.

Why this matters: a non-profit serving 150 patients with cancer-related neuropathy needs a different cannabinoid profile than one serving 150 patients with treatment-resistant epilepsy or chronic pain. The Plant Library lets us match parcels to specific therapeutic targets — and accumulate, year over year, a library of stable genetics + proven cultivation protocols + clinical observations that compounds in value.

This is the asset that competitors with biotech-fermentation models structurally cannot replicate. They produce one molecule at high purity. We produce a portfolio of full-spectrum extracts, each tied to a specific patient cohort and clinical question.

07 / Team

Three founders. Aligned on biodynamic + R&D.

Loma Verde is a three-founder partnership with complementary backgrounds in operations, agriculture, and finance. Detailed CVs available on request post-NDA. Lead operator is Vic, a part-time founder with structured 25-hour weekly commitment and a contracted Chief of Staff handling delegation.

Equity and commitment

All three founders contribute USD 30,000 each (USD 90K total) as irrevocable equity capital at round close. This is real money on the same terms — not founder shares in exchange for sweat equity. We believe an investor's first question on any cannabis project should be: how much of their own cash is the team putting in? Our answer is 36% of the round.

Operating model

Day-to-day operations are run by an on-site team contracted under Argentina's UATRE agricultural framework: a foreman, four base operators, and a fractional agronomist transitioning to full-time by month 12. A Chief of Staff coordinates between the three founders and reports operational metrics to the investor pool quarterly.

The 180-day post-funding plan (available as a separate operational document) details week-by-week milestones, hiring targets, KPI cadence, and contingency plans for the most likely operational risks. It is not aspirational — it is a working tool already being executed against by the operating team.

Risk we own

Argentina is not a politically stable jurisdiction. The 2025 dissolution of ARICCAME demonstrated that. Our specific channel — patient registry under the Ministry of Health — has remained operational throughout political transitions because it serves an established patient base with formal medical credentialing. But this is Argentina, and a US investor needs to understand: the regulatory environment can shift faster than in most OECD countries.

Our mitigation is structural: we operate through the most institutionally protected channel (registered patients with prescribing physicians), our CAPEX is paid for in pesos against USD-denominated revenue (natural FX hedge), and our service-only structure means we do not hold inventory, do not own product, and do not depend on export licensing to monetize.
